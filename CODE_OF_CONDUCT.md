# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We — contributors and maintainers of Crea8iv PatientFlow — pledge to make participation in this project a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Because this project handles **clinic and patient data**, we hold an additional pledge: to treat all data we encounter with the confidentiality and care that medical information deserves.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users (clinics and their patients).
- Showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:
- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments; personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- **Accessing, copying, sharing, or exposing real patient/clinic data** outside what a task strictly requires.
- Introducing code that intentionally weakens security or data isolation.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Data Responsibility

- Never use real patient data for testing or demos — use the Demo clinic / synthetic data.
- Never commit secrets, dumps, or uploads.
- Report any accidental exposure of sensitive data immediately and privately (see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)).

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior — or mishandling of data — may be reported to the project maintainers (Crea8iv Media). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face repercussions as determined by the project's leadership.
